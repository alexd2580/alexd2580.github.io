About Me

I am a software developer. Starting with a programmable calculator and very simple procedural programms I made my way through different different languages, paradigms and development environments.

One of the most interesting parts of software development for me is watching a project grow, as new features are added, while the existing ones are optimized and tuned. Thus the code needs to be well structured and properly documented, so that bugs can be taken care of as fast as possible

In addition to my professional life I also spend a lot of my free time working on small projects, as well as discussing engineering problems with friends. A lot of them have turned into contests with the goal being efficiency, speed, or code simplicity. For example autonomous agents for games. In general, developing algorithms for solving problems of the current century and challenges of modern, cloud-based and high-scale platforms is a great passion to me

My preferred development platform is Arch Linux, with its tight cooperation with open source communities. With Linux being one of the most popular server operating systems and also facing the growing cross-platform market, Linux knowledge is an important asset in the software market.

I consider myself capable of not only operating on and deploying to conventional bare-metal systems but also to container platforms such as Docker. In the past I have configured Continuous Integration as well as Continuous Delivery for multiple customers.

The study at the TUHH in Hamburg-Harburg has taught me the valuable lesson of never taking anything as granted, especially in the world of software, as well as broad knowledge of computer theory, architectures, distributed systems and communication protocols.

I am used to working in large teams, which would be unimaginable without version control systems such as Git or SVN.
