About Me

I am a software developer. Starting with a programmable calculator and very simple procedural programms I made my way through different different languages, paradigms and development environments.

One of the most interesting parts of software development for me is watching a project grow, as new features are added, while the existing ones are optimized and tuned. Thus the code needs to be well structured and properly documented, so that bugs can be taken care of as fast as possible

I spend a lot of my free time working on small projects, which are usually based on theoretical discussions with friends. A lot of them have turned into contests with the goal being efficiency, speed, or code simplicity. For example autonomous agents for specific games, or graphical crossword solvers. Game development in general is one of my favorite domains.

My preferred development platform is Linux, with its tight cooperation with open source communities. Being one of the most popular server operating systems and also facing the growing cross-platform market, especially for games, Linux knowledge is an important asset in the software market.

The study at the TUHH in Hamburg-Harburg has taught me the valuable lesson of never taking anything as granted, especially in the world of software, as well as broad knowledge of computer theory, architectures, distributed systems and communication protocols.

Most projects I worked on were group projects, which would be unimaginable without version control systems such as Git or SVN.
